What Is It?

The George R. Brown Convention Center is a large building in downtown Houston that is used for events. People go there for conventions, expos, meetings, festivals, and different public events.

History

The convention center opened in 1987. It was named after George Rufus Brown, who was a Houston businessman and civic leader. Over time, the building became one of the main event locations in the city.
